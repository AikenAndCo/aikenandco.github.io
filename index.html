Revenue Growth Management

I work in Revenue Growth Management. What I've found over the last few years is that AI changes what's actually possible in this kind of work. Not the version people put in press releases. The version where analysis that used to take a week takes an afternoon, where a pricing tool that used to need a data science team gets built by one person over a weekend, and where the question shifts from "can we do this" to "what should we do with it."

I learned to speak those languages by doing all of them. Retail. Consumer banking. Accounting. FP&A. Brand finance. Commercial sales finance. Sales analytics. Account management. And RGM at L'Oréal, Coca-Cola, and now Revlon. Most of those conversations need a translator. Sometimes I'm the translator.

My favorite thing is teaching this stuff. On a whiteboard, in a meeting, or with a lemonade stand. Turns out it's a surprisingly good model for almost any pricing, promo, or financial concept you'd want to explain to a CFO, a buyer, or an eight-year-old.

Off the clock: a camera. A kitchen. A dog. A story to tell.
